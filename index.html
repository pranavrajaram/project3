Where's My Water? How Different Body Parts React to Dehydration

Drag the slider and hover over body parts to see how running on a treadmill impacts the body!

Experiment Context:

This visualization represents how total body water (TBW) loss occurs over time during exercise.

  • Participants ran on a treadmill for 120 minutes without fluid intake, divided into 8 running intervals (15 min each).
  • After each running interval, there was an 8-minute rest where bioimpedance, temperature, sweat, and saliva samples were collected.
  • TBW loss was estimated using weight changes measured by a high-precision scale (±5g accuracy).
  • Bioimpedance data was collected at different body parts, with higher impedance indicating lower water content.

Key Observations:

  • 📉 Right Arm Shows the Most Water Loss: Impedance decreased by ~3.4%, indicating high dehydration.
  • 🛡️ The Trunk Shows the Least Water Loss: Impedance slightly increased (~1.2%), suggesting water redistribution.
  • 💧 Limbs Lose More Water Than the Core: Peripheral areas (arms and legs) experienced more dehydration than the trunk.
  • 🔄 Water Shifts to the Trunk: The body appears to retain water in the core to protect vital organs.
