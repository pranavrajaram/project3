Where's My Water? How Different Body Parts React to Dehydration

Drag the slider and hover over body parts to see how running on a treadmill impacts the body!

Additional Information:

Participants ran on a treadmill for 120 minutes with bioimpedance data taken in 15 minute intervals.

Key Observations:

- Limbs lose more water as bioimpedance increases over time.

- Trunk shows increased water content (decreased impedance) due to the body's overcorrection.

- Water shifts in the body to protect vital organs.
